# Model Comparison

 

## Purpose 

This document compares simple regression models and the multiple regression model to understand what drives monthly sales.

 

---

 

## Model Comparison Table

 

| Model Name | Variables Used | R-squared | Significant Variables | Business Usefulness |

|------------|--------------|----------|----------------------|---------------------|

| Simple Model 1 | marketing_spend | Moderate | marketing_spend | Useful for basic understanding |

| Simple Model 2 | footfall | High | footfall | Strong relationship |

| Simple Model 3 | inventory_availability | Moderate | inventory_availability | Important operational driver |

| Multiple Regression | marketing_spend, footfall, inventory, discount, staff, rating, store type | Very High | multiple variables | Best model |

 

---

 

## Interpretation 

 

Simple regression helps understand individual relationships, but it ignores combined effects.

 

The multiple regression model explains sales better because it includes several business drivers together.

 

---

 

## Final Model Selected 

 

Multiple Regression Model is selected because:

- Higher R-squared 

- Multiple influencing factors included 

- Better business relevance 

 

---

 

## Limitations 

 

- Does not include external factors 

- Shows association, not causation 

- Some variables may overlap in effect
